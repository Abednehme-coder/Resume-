Hobbies

If you had asked me why I chose computer engineering back in the day, I would have told you it was because I found it interesting.
But now, I see it as a lifestyle. I feel lucky to have discovered what makes me happy early on in life.
There’s an underrated thrill in being curious and embracing every possible path along this journey—a feeling that perfectly sums up my interests in the digital world.
But the thing that initially caught my attention was AI, and I honestly want to benefit from AI myself. Just as my professors taught me how to code, helped me choose the best algorithms, and explained why they work, I want to teach AI in a similar fashion,
not just let it consume all the data on the web. Looking at how these complex modules think about a single problem is fascinating to me, and I want to invest in that.

Besides that, here are some of the things I enjoy right now:

  • Coding.
  • Weightlifting and sports in general.
  • Consuming general knowledge from documentaries and movies (this gives me a huge social push, as it enables me to communicate with people from outside my domain).
  • Gaming.
  • Scouts.
  • If you’ve ever come across the term "pipelining," I’m obsessed with it.
